# 🍔 McDonald’s Global Nutrition & Strategy Analysis  
### India vs USA — A Cross-Market Data & Strategy Deep Dive

---
👉 View the live McDonald’s Global Nutrition Dashboard: https://mcdonalds-menu-america-vs-india.lovable.app
## 🧠 Project Overview

This project analyzes McDonald’s menu architecture across **India and the United States** using a combination of **SQL exploration, Python analysis, and cross-market comparison modeling**.

The goal is to understand how **culture, competition, and market maturity** shape:

- Menu composition
- Nutritional profiles
- Health risk distribution
- Strategic positioning of McDonald’s across markets

Rather than a simple nutritional breakdown, this project frames McDonald’s as a **global system adapting to local constraints while maintaining a consistent profitability model**.

---

## 🎯 Key Question

> How do cultural, religious, and competitive market structures shape McDonald’s menu design and nutritional outcomes across India and the USA?

## 🔍 Key Areas of Analysis

### 1. 🌍 Market Structure & Competition
- India: fragmented QSR market (Domino’s, KFC, Burger King competition)
- USA: consolidated market led by McDonald’s (26% share)
- Market structure directly influences menu design and localization strategy

---

### 2. 🍔 Menu Composition Differences
- India:
  - High vegetarian + paneer-based adaptation
  - Strong McCafé presence
- USA:
  - Protein-heavy (beef, chicken, pork)
  - Larger breakfast segmentation
  - Higher category specialization

---

### 3. 🧪 Nutrition Architecture
- USA menu is significantly more:
  - Calorie-dense
  - Protein-heavy
  - Portion-driven
- India menu shows:
  - Lower calorie density
  - Higher variability due to localization
  - Sugar concentrated in beverages (McCafé)

---

### 4. 🌱 Veg vs Non-Veg (India Insight)
- Veg ≠ automatically healthy
- Veg items often:
  - Lower protein density
  - Moderate to high calorie load (paneer, fried items, sauces)
- Non-veg items provide:
  - Higher protein efficiency
  - But higher sodium/fat tradeoffs

---

### 5. ☕ McCafé Strategy (Global Theme)
- McCafé is a **high-margin strategic pillar**
- Competes indirectly with:
  - Starbucks
  - Costa Coffee
- Represents McDonald’s shift toward a:
> “QSR + Café hybrid model”

- Strong presence in both India and USA menus

---

### 6. ⚠️ Health Risk & Outliers
- Extreme calorie items concentrated in:
  - USA breakfast meals
  - Large combo portions
- Risk drivers:
  - Sodium (savory items)
  - Sugar (beverages & desserts)
  - Saturated fat (fried chicken & beef)

---

### 7. 📊 Cross-Market Insights
- USA has:
  - ~47% higher calorie density
  - ~3x sugar levels
  - Higher protein concentration
- India has:
  - Lower calorie density
  - Stronger cultural constraints shaping protein sources
  - Higher dependence on McCafé for revenue structure

---

## 📈 Key Insights (Executive Summary)

- McDonald’s is not a burger company — it is a **localized nutrition platform**
- Menu structure is driven more by **culture and competition than by nutrition logic**
- McCafé is a **global margin expansion strategy**
- USA = scale + protein intensity  
- India = localization + cultural adaptation
- Health outcomes are a **byproduct of business structure, not design accident**

---

## 🧠 Strategic Takeaway

> McDonald’s global success is not built on menu consistency — but on systematic adaptation of food architecture to cultural and competitive environments while preserving a unified profitability model.
